Northern European Destinations

Although there are many countries to choose from in Northern Europe, this will focus on the areas that the editors know best and try to offer tips for destinations that are not included on every other Top 10 List.

The Netherlands

Amsterdam

Amsterdam

For most tourists, their first experience of the Netherlands is Amsterdam, which happens to be a truly beautiful city that is small enough that you can easily walk or cycle to any of the neighborhoods and tourist sites. So go ahead and tire out your legs and feet at art museums, breweries, coffeeshops, gaping at prostitutes in the red light district - no one's here to judge - and be sure to take the time to stop by the small, canalside cafes and bars to have a coffee or beer and watch the canal boats and people. And don't skip a cruise along the canals. Whether you end up on a big tourist boat or are lucky enough to cruise on a smaller canal boat, it's a wonderful way to see the city.

Koningsdag

Koningsdag

If you happen to be in Amsterdam on April 27, which is King's Day, be prepared for the biggest orange-themed street and canal party imaginable. It is a national holiday and all businesses close and everyone floods the city wearing orange. It feels like Amsterdam decided to throw a party and everyone came. Have you ever used a public toilet on the deck of a canal boat while house music blares from giant speakers? You may have after spending King's Day in Amsterdam.

The Kröller-Müller Museum

The Kröller-Müller Museum

Don't forget to look outside of Amsterdam! There are great day trips from Amsterdam that are only a short train journey away. If the weather forecast is good, definitely consider getting the train to Apeldorn and heading over the the The Kröller-Müller Museum located in The Hoge Veluwe National Park. It is vast expanse of changing landscapes with areas of green grasss bordered by gently rolling sand dunes. Admission includes the use of a fleet of white bicycles so that you can fully access the sculpture garden, art museum, and national park. You just park a bike anywhere you want to stop and then grab another one when you want to ride again.

England

Oxford and beyond

Oxford and beyond

If you are interested in seeing more of England than just London, Oxford is a good choice for getting out of the city. Oxford itself is quite small, but very beautiful and easily navigated by tourists. All of the historic colleges that make up the university are in the city center, as are numerous historic pubs, museums, and cafes. The Weston Library was recently revamped and now has wonderful exhibitions of rare books and manuscripts and a particularly good cafe in the lobby.

Ascott House

Ascott House

Just north of London and east of Oxford is Ascott House, a Jacobean timbered farmhouse that was a country retreat of the de Rothschilds family before becoming a National Trust property. It houses a fine collection of art and sits amongst beautifully planted grounds that are open to visitors.

Kelmscott Manor

Kelmscott Manor

A short drive west of Oxford is Kelmscott Manor, the former country house of William Morris. The house is filled with furniture and furnishings from the arts and crafts period as well as a fine collection of art, most noteworthy being a portrait of Jane Morris by Dante Gabiel Rossetti titled Blue Silk Dress.

Scotland

Edinburgh

Edinburgh

Edinburgh is a beautiful, historic city packed with art galleries, museums, Holyrood Palace, and numerous other destinations. One of the lesser-known museums is the recently renovated Surgeons' Hall Museums. Edinburgh has long been associated with medical studies and the Royal College of Surgeons of Edinburgh was founded in 1505. This museum is located in the city center and encompasses a pathology museum, surgery museum, and dental collectin and is well worth a visit. If you fancy a curry after your visit to the museum, the low-key favorite of many locals is just around the corner at Kebab Mahal, or for slightly more upscale, vegetarian cuisine, Kalpna is only a few blocks away.

Abbotsford

Abbotsford

Abbotsford was the 19th century home of Sir Walter Scott on the banks of the river Tweed in the Borders region of Scotland that is southeast of Edinburgh. The house is filled with original furnishings and mementoes from Scott and is situated amongst beautiful gardens that are open to visitors. If you are particularly flush, one wing of the house is available as luxury accomodations.

Lochleven Castle

Lochleven Castle

Lochleven Castle is a stone castle built around 1300 on a small island in Loch Leven, north of Edinburgh and across the Firth of Forth. Many famous Scots were held here along the centuries, the most famous being Mary Queen of Scots. In order to access the island and castle, you must ride in a small boat that ferries visitors back and forth to the island. Once there, the ruins of the castle can be toured, the island can be explored, and there are areas for picnics.

Iceland

The Blue Lagoon

The Blue Lagoon

Almost every tourist arrives and departs Iceland through Keflavik airport. One of the glorious benefits of this is that you can arrange to visit the Blue Lagoon Geothermal Spa on your way from the airport to your accomodations after you arrive or on the way to the airport before you depart. Not only are the spa's geothermal pools and silica masks soothing, there is also a sauna and steam room, relaxation areas, and a restaurant. And all of it is set in a truly beautiful and unique landscape.

Day Tours of Iceland

Day Tours of Iceland

Several companies operate tours that leave Reykjavik in the morning and take tourists to different areas of Iceland. The Golden Circle Tour is one of the most popular and allows you to see Gullfoss Waterfall, geysirs, Icelandic horses, and lovely scenery. There are also tours of other areas, such as the southern coast, where you will see glaciers, waterfalls, impressve rocky formations on the coastline, and Skógar Folk Museum. The scenery of Iceland is truly unique and any of the tours of the country are well worth it.
